Golden Triangle Travel Guides


Delhi Sightseeing on the Golden Triangle Tour

A visit to Delhi is a must when exploring the Golden Triangle Tour, which comprises three iconic destinations in India. The city is a perfect blend of the old and new, featuring a wealth of historic monuments, modern markets, shopping malls, restaurants, lounges, and amusement parks that offer something for everyone. Stroll through the chaotic streets of Chandani Chowk, marvel at the colossal Jama Masjid, climb up the towering Qutub Minar, and explore the ruins at Mehrauli for a glimpse into the city's rich history. In contrast, the circular market of Connaught Place, the imperial Rajpath, and the arched India Gate showcase the city's diversity and modernity. Delhi is also home to many parks, gardens, museums, stadiums, and cultural centers, making it an essential destination of the Golden Triangle Tour. Whether you're a history buff or a modern-day explorer, Delhi has something unique to offer that will make your Golden Triangle Tour a truly golden experience.


Agra Sightseeing on the Golden Triangle Tour

Agra, the proud abode of the 'Symbol of Love' - Taj Mahal, takes you on a journey back to the Mughal era. Dotted with forts, palaces, mausoleums, and mosques, Agra showcases the deep love of Mughals for art and architecture. Ride a camel to the entrance of the majestic Taj Mahal and witness the tale of undying love it tells. Step through the massive doors of Agra Fort that once housed the Mughals. Pay homage to one of the country's greatest rulers, Akbar, at his resting place in Sikandra. And, most importantly, behold the magnificent Fatehpur Sikri, a walled city with palaces, gardens, and mosques that has undoubtedly put Agra on the map.


Jaipur Sightseeing on Golden Triangle Tour

Jaipur, the capital of a royal and culturally rich state, embodies everything one would expect. As the third leg of the Golden Triangle Tour, Jaipur boasts gigantic forts and palaces with exquisite architecture, temples that attract the religiously inclined, and a vibrant culture that cannot be ignored. The city showcases the many flavors of Rajasthan, from handicrafts to architecture, and from traditions to cuisine; everything in the city is worth experiencing and witnessing. For history enthusiasts, there are forts like Amer and Nahargarh, while shopaholics can explore traditional bazaars, and food lovers can indulge in the culinary delights of Chokhi Dhaani. As the capital city, Jaipur is also infused with modern flavors, with malls, cafes, lounges, and boutiques dotting the cityscape.


The Best Time to Take the India Golden Triangle Tour

While the three destinations welcome travelers throughout the year, it is ideal to plan a Golden Triangle Tour between September and March. The weather during these months remains pleasant and adds to the charm of exploring.
